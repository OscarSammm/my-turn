Soccer

My Game. My Journey. My Standard.

February 21, 2026

I’m Toro — a student-athlete and coach. This page is my full soccer story: where I’ve played, what I’ve earned, how I train, and why my standard is different.

Quick Snapshot

I play with purpose. I’m the type of player who wants the ball in difficult moments, and I work every day to be ready when that moment comes.

  • Name: Toro
  • Positions: Winger / CAM
  • Strengths: 1v1, pace, finishing, work rate, game IQ
  • Style: Direct, aggressive, disciplined, and team-first

Journey

Soccer has been the one thing I never stopped building. The journey isn’t just about goals or highlights — it’s about the mindset I developed: show up, work, learn, repeat.

I came from Honduras with big goals and I knew I had to earn everything. I learned fast that talent gets you noticed, but habits keep you there. So I built habits.

Every season I focused on one thing: becoming more complete. Not only scoring, but also defending, pressing, communicating, and making teammates better.

Where I’ve Played

I’ve competed across multiple levels, and each level forced me to grow. Different coaches, different teammates, different systems — I learned how to adapt while keeping my identity.

  • Cypress Creek High School — District Champions (Senior Year), scored 22 goals
  • Paris Junior College — Freshman/Sophomore, 18 goals + 13 assists, Second Team All-Region
  • UT Tyler — Sophomore/Junior
  • TAMUSA — Current

I’ve played both wide and central. I can attack from the wing with pace and 1v1 skill, or create in the middle as a CAM with timing, vision, and quick decision-making.

Personal Recognitions & Wins

I’m proud of the results, but I’m more proud of the consistency behind them. These are outcomes of discipline — not luck.

  • District Champion (High School)
  • 22 goals (Senior Year)
  • 18 goals + 13 assists (Paris Junior College)
  • Second Team All-Region
  • Player of the Game / Player of the Season
  • Region All-Team / Conference All-Team

For me, recognition is a reminder: keep the same hunger, even when things are going well.

My Playing Identity

I play fast and fearless, but controlled. My goal is to create danger — either by beating my man, combining quickly, or arriving at the right time in the box.

What I bring on the field

  • 1v1 threat: I attack space, force mistakes, and commit defenders
  • End product: I look for the final pass or finish with confidence
  • Work rate: I press, track back, and do the dirty work
  • Decision-making: I keep it simple when it’s needed
  • Mentality: I don’t disappear when the game gets hard

What I’m always improving

  • Faster scanning and earlier decisions
  • Finishing under pressure and from different angles
  • More control of tempo (when to slow down vs. speed up)

Why I Know I’m Different

My confidence comes from work. I don’t rely on motivation — I rely on routine. When I say I’m different, I mean I’m consistent.

I train when I’m tired. I review my mistakes. I ask questions. I stay disciplined with recovery and sleep. I show up ready, because I respect the game.

  • Same standard every day: practice, gym, film, recovery
  • Coachability: I want feedback, even if it’s hard
  • Leadership: I bring energy and accountability
  • Big-moment mindset: pressure makes me focus more, not less

How I Train

I don’t train randomly. I train with intention. I build the athlete first, then sharpen the soccer tools on top of that.

Technical work

  • Ball mastery + first touch (both feet)
  • 1v1 moves + change of pace
  • Finishing: near post / far post / cutbacks / first time
  • Passing + receiving under pressure

Physical work

  • Acceleration + top speed mechanics
  • Agility and quick direction changes
  • Strength training for power and injury prevention
  • Conditioning that matches game demands (intervals, tempo)

Mental + tactical

  • Scanning habits (checking shoulders)
  • Understanding space and timing runs
  • Staying calm and making good decisions under pressure

How I Coach (1-on-1)

When I coach, I keep it simple: I fix one thing at a time and build confidence through reps. The goal is performance — not just a hard workout.

What sessions usually include

  • Warm-up + mobility
  • Technical foundation (touch, turns, control)
  • Game-speed drills (1v1, finishing, decision-making)
  • Short conditioning finisher
  • Quick feedback + next focus

If you’re serious, I’ll push you — but I’ll also teach you how to train the right way so you can improve faster.
