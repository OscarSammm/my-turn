Running

My Engine. My Discipline. My Finish.

February 27, 2026

Running is where I build my engine. This page is my running mindset: how I train speed and endurance, how I stay consistent, and why this part of my routine makes me a better athlete.

Quick Snapshot

I don’t run “just to run.” I run with purpose. Every session has a reason: speed, conditioning, recovery, or mental toughness.

  • Focus: Speed + endurance
  • Style: Consistent, controlled, disciplined
  • Strengths: tempo control, repeat effort, strong finish
  • Why it matters: better engine, better recovery, better performance

Journey

Running taught me patience. It’s not about one perfect day — it’s about stacking good weeks. That mindset helped me in soccer, school, and everything else.

Over time I learned how to listen to my body, stay consistent, and still push when it’s time to push. That balance is the key.

Why I Run

Running is the foundation of my conditioning. It builds the engine that lets me press, recover faster, and stay sharp late in the game.

  • Endurance: I can keep my level high for longer
  • Mental toughness: I don’t quit when it gets hard
  • Recovery: easy runs help me stay fresh
  • Confidence: I trust my legs in the final minutes

How I Train

I keep it simple: a mix of easy runs, tempo work, intervals, and recovery. I don’t chase random workouts — I build progression.

Speed work

  • Short intervals (fast reps, full effort)
  • Hills for power and mechanics
  • Strides to stay sharp

Endurance work

  • Easy aerobic runs (base + recovery)
  • Tempo runs (controlled discomfort)
  • Longer steady efforts (engine building)

My Standards

Running is where I prove consistency to myself. Motivation comes and goes, but my standard stays the same.

  • Consistency: I show up even on low days
  • Form: I run smooth, not sloppy
  • Recovery: sleep + mobility are training
  • Progress: I track what matters

How I Coach Running

If you want to get faster or build conditioning, I coach with the same approach I use: structure, progression, and confidence.

Sessions usually include

  • Warm-up + mechanics (posture, cadence, breathing)
  • Speed or tempo work (based on your level)
  • Short finisher + cooldown
  • Feedback + next focus
