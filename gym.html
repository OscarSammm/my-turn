Gym

Strength. Power. Durability.

February 27, 2026

The gym is where I build the athlete behind the skill. This page is my training philosophy: strength that transfers, power that shows on the field, and durability that keeps me consistent.

Quick Snapshot

I train for performance, not ego. My goal is to move better, get stronger, and stay healthy — so my game and my body can handle pressure.

  • Focus: Strength + power + durability
  • Style: clean form, progressive overload, consistent
  • Strengths: lower-body power, core stability, athletic movement
  • Why it matters: faster, stronger, harder to break

Journey

I learned that skill is not enough if your body can’t support it. The gym helped me become more durable, more explosive, and more confident in contact and high-speed moments.

Over time I stopped lifting randomly and started training with a plan: build the base, add power, then maintain and stay fresh in season.

Why I Lift

The gym is where I build the qualities that transfer: acceleration, balance, strength in duels, and injury resistance.

  • Power: first steps, jumps, quick bursts
  • Strength: hold off defenders, win duels
  • Stability: control through contact
  • Injury prevention: hips, knees, ankles, core

How I Train

I keep it simple and athletic. Big movements, clean reps, and smart volume. I want strength that shows up in real movement.

Foundation

  • Squat + hinge patterns (legs + posterior chain)
  • Push/pull balance (upper body strength)
  • Core stability (anti-rotation, control)

Power + athletic work

  • Explosive jumps and sprints
  • Medicine ball throws (rotation + intent)
  • Single-leg strength (balance + transfer)

Mobility + recovery

  • Hips/ankles mobility
  • Prehab for knees and hamstrings
  • Sleep + nutrition + consistency

My Standards

The gym is where discipline is obvious. You either show up and do the reps right, or you don’t. I choose the standard.

  • Form first: quality reps only
  • No ego: progress beats showing off
  • Balance: strength + mobility
  • Recovery: I protect my body

How I Coach Gym Training

I coach gym work the same way I train: simple structure, strong basics, and progression that fits your sport and level.

Sessions usually include

  • Warm-up + mobility
  • Strength block (main lifts)
  • Athletic power work (jumps/throws/sprints)
  • Core + durability finisher
  • Feedback + next goal

Proof & Progress

I’m always tracking progress: strength numbers, body quality, and how it transfers to performance. If you want a plan, message me.
